US-Israel-Iran War News Live Updates: Iran says US presence in cities led to urban-area strikes in Gulf
The conflict between Israel and Iran continues, with intensifying strikes, targeted killings, and a widening regional footprint that stretches across multiple countries. What began as coordinated military action has evolved into a multi-front war involving state and non-state actors, raising fears of a prolonged and destabilising crisis in the Middle East.

A major turning point came when Israel confirmed the killing of senior Iranian security official Ali Larijani and Revolutionary Guard commander Gholam Reza Soleimani in precision overnight strikes. Both men were deeply embedded in Iran’s power structure and were seen as key figures in maintaining internal control, especially during recent anti-government protests. Their deaths mark a significant escalation in Israel’s strategy, directly targeting Iran’s leadership network.

Iran responded swiftly, launching waves of missile and drone attacks aimed at Israeli cities and critical infrastructure across the Gulf. The use of multiple-warhead missiles signals an attempt to overwhelm defence systems and maximise damage. As the conflict deepens, neighbouring countries like Lebanon and Iraq have been pulled further into the crisis. Israeli strikes have expanded into central Beirut, hitting residential areas and causing civilian casualties, while Hezbollah continues sustained rocket fire into northern Israel.

Israeli forces also carried out fresh strikes in Lebanon, targeting the town of Sahmar in the eastern Bekaa Valley for the second time this morning. The attack followed two earlier raids on Yahmar, also in the Bekaa Valley.

Meanwhile, Kuwait’s National Guard said it shot down seven drones at dawn, hours after the army reported intercepting hostile missile and drone activity. Qatar’s armed forces also intercepted a missile targeting the country, its Ministry of Defence said.

The United States military remains directly involved, with its presence in the region increasingly under threat. A drone strike on the US Embassy compound in Baghdad triggered fires. Saudi Arabia, meanwhile, intercepted a ballistic missile near an air base hosting US troops, highlighting how the conflict is spilling into key strategic zones.

The humanitarian situation is deteriorating rapidly. Thousands have been killed or injured, and more than a million people have been displaced, particularly in Lebanon. Healthcare systems are under strain, and access to basic services is becoming increasingly difficult. Concerns have also been raised about nuclear safety after reports of a projectile striking Iran’s Bushehr nuclear complex, though no major damage has been confirmed so far.

As fire and fury hit MAGA tent, Vance walks the tightrope for 2028 
Premium
The new war launched by U.S. President Donald Trump and Benjamin Netanyahu is triggering a wide range of realignment of personal loyalties and political positions across both parties, but a role reversal within the Republican Party is remarkable.
Updated - March 18, 2026 12:44 pm IST

Varghese K  George Varghese K. George
Google Preferred Source
U.S. President Donald Trump, Vice-President J.D. Vance and Secretary of State Marco Rubio take part in a meeting with Irish Taoiseach (Prime Minister) Micheal Martin at the White House in Washington, D.C., U.S. on March 17, 2026.
U.S. President Donald Trump, Vice-President J.D. Vance and Secretary of State Marco Rubio take part in a meeting with Irish Taoiseach (Prime Minister) Micheal Martin at the White House in Washington, D.C., U.S. on March 17, 2026. | Photo Credit: Reuters

The fire and fury that is consuming the MAGA (Make America Great Again) fraternity that had rallied behind Donald J. Trump’s anti-war rhetoric for a decade touched the administration on Tuesday (March 17, 2026) when Joe Kent, a long-term acolyte, resigned as the director of the National Counterterrorism Centre. “Iran posed no imminent threat to our nation, and it is clear that we started this war due to pressure from Israel and its powerful American lobby,” Mr. Kent, who served in the U.S. Army for 20 years and later in the CIA, wrote in his resignation letter.

Laura Loomer, an influential cheerleader of the war who has Mr. Trump’s ear took to social media speculating that Tulsi Gabbard, Director of National Intelligence and a strong opponent of wars, could be the next to resign.
